Gender matters

Each suicide is a tragedy. Every 40 seconds a person dies from suicide somewhere in the world. The estimated global burden of suicide is over 800,000 deaths per year. Suicide accounted for total mortality from cancer, heart disease, AIDS, birth defects, stroke, pneumonia, influenza, and chronic lung disease, combined. These figures underestimate the problem. For each suicide related death, there are approximately 10 to 40 attempted suicides In addition, a lot of people with suicidal thoughts never seek services. Suicides occur in all parts of the world and throughout the lifespan. It is the second leading cause of death in young people 15 to 29 years of age and is highest in persons aged 70 years or over for both men and women in most regions of the world . Suicide is one of the greatest sources of premature death.

The availability of YRBS allows us to examine suicidal patterns in teens, a population that seems to be exponentially climbing the mortality ladder in the past few years. Data collected by YRBS has about 5% of cells contain missing, but about 59% of respondents provided at least one missing response. The large portion of missings led to imputation, but the data eventually reveal the impact of Gender as a group to teen suicidal behavior. Female predominance in the gender group allows a closer look to distribution by the most significant determinants of suicidal attempt:

  • Drug
  • Alcohol
  • Tobacco Use
  • Behavior, social, emotional patterns

While male tend to display extreme contrast between groups such as by the impact of threats, frequency of gun carry and engagement in fights. Female teen respondents display more ambiguous pattern of social behavior despite of higher likelihood of attempt suicide. This indicates the difficulty is defining the determining factor of suicide among teens. When respondents/afflicted are less willing to reveal actual feelings due to fear of judgements. Especially among the sexually abused groups.

To have more accurate analysis on Race, we will need to consider to weight the biased distribution. However, by examining serious suicide incident we are able to discover Asians are the most prone to commit suicide result in injury comparing other minority groups

An individual's BMI displays direct correlation with the likelihood of suicide attempt. The population with higher BMI is more prone to suicide risk.

Future studies on the cause and signs of teen suicide are necessary to stop this pre-matured death from climbing. Any loss of life, especially that of a young person, is tragic and worth to be informed of possible causes.
