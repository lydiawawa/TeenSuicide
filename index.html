This site is dedicated to portray the common characteristics of suicide victims by using data visualization combined with quantitative research. The purpose of the research is to serve as a preventative measurement to suicides,especially among teens.

Before examining the common traits of teen suicide victims, it is necessary to explore its overall distribution in the total population. This section focuses on suicide in the states among all ages to start with the bigger picture of suicide victim distribution.

Suicide Mortality by State in 2017

In 2017, the state with lowest suicide is New York, the low rate is most likely driven by its large population. On the other end, Montana claims the first spot to have a 28.9 state wide suicide rate, followed closely by Alaska with a rate of 27%.

Age-Adjusted Suicide Rates in the United States (2001-2017)

Suicide rate is based on the number of people who have died by suicide per 100,000 population. Because changes in population size are taken into account, rates allow for comparisons from one year to the next.

Figure above shows age-adjusted suicide rate of the total population for female and male represented seperately.
