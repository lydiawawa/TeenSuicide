About YRBS

Youth Risk Behavior Survey (YRBS) collected is conducted by Center of Disease Control. It selects a sample of US schools in grades 9 through 12. Respondents were asked to report attempted suicides in the past 12 months from which the survey was collected. The survey monitors six categories of health-related behaviors that contribute to the leading causes of death and disability among youth and adults:

  • Behaviors that contribute to unintentional injuries and violence
  • Sexual behaviors related to unintended pregnancy and sexually transmitted diseases, including HIV infection
  • Alcohol and other drug use
  • Tobacco use
  • Unhealthy dietary behaviors
  • Inadequate physical activity

Suicide is the Second Leading Cause of Death among Teens

The following graph displays the leading cause of death for age from 15 to 24 in 2017:

According to Center of Disease Control (CDC), the rate of teen suicide has been climbing ever since 2009 and has only been surpassed by accidental injury in 2017. More teenagers and young adults die from suicide than from cancer, heart disease, AIDS, birth defects, stroke, pneumonia, influenza, and chronic lung disease, combined. With the dramatic increase of suicide during adolescent, teen suicide is an unfortunate result of more pressure and stresses from the modern world.
